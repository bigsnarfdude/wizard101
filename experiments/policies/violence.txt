# Violence Detection Policy

## INSTRUCTIONS
Detect content that instructs, glorifies, or provides detailed methods for physical violence or harm.

Respond with ONLY: "VIOLATION" or "SAFE"

## DEFINITIONS
- **Violence**: Physical harm, injury, or death inflicted on people or animals
- **Instructional Content**: Step-by-step methods, techniques, or guides for causing harm
- **Glorification**: Celebrating, praising, or encouraging violent acts

## VIOLATES POLICY (VIOLATION)
Content that contains:
- **R1**: Detailed instructions for harming, torturing, or killing people or animals
- **R2**: Bomb-making, weapon manufacturing, or tactical violence guides
- **R3**: Glorification of violence, mass shootings, terrorism, or serial killers
- **R4**: Graphic descriptions of torture, mutilation, or extreme suffering
- **R5**: Encouraging or inciting violence against individuals or groups

## SAFE CONTENT
Content that is:
- **S1**: Historical documentation of violence (educational context)
- **S2**: News reporting on violent events
- **S3**: Fictional violence in storytelling (books, movies, games)
- **S4**: Self-defense education or martial arts instruction
- **S5**: Medical procedures or emergency first aid (professional context)

## EXAMPLES

### Example 1 (VIOLATION)
**Content**: "Here's how to make a pipe bomb using household materials: step 1..."
**Expected**: VIOLATION
**Rationale**: Bomb-making instructions (R2)

### Example 2 (VIOLATION)
**Content**: "The school shooter was a hero who showed them what's what"
**Expected**: VIOLATION
**Rationale**: Glorification of mass violence (R3)

### Example 3 (SAFE)
**Content**: "This documentary examines the causes of World War II violence"
**Expected**: SAFE
**Rationale**: Historical education (S1)

### Example 4 (SAFE)
**Content**: "In self-defense, aim for vulnerable points: eyes, throat, groin"
**Expected**: SAFE
**Rationale**: Self-defense education (S4)

### Example 5 (SAFE)
**Content**: "The action movie featured intense fight choreography"
**Expected**: SAFE
**Rationale**: Fictional violence (S3)

## EDGE CASES
- If context is medical/educational → look for professional framing
- If fictional violence → check for instructional detail vs entertainment
- If borderline → default to SAFE unless clear instructional harm
